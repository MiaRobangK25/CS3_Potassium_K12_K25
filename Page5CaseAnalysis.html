Case Analysis


MOTION: This house regrets the narrative that 'life goes on'
SIDE: Government / Proposition
Debate Format: Asian Parliamentary

Framing:

  • What is the narrative?
    • Justify problems you have as “natural” and part of life
    • Generally nonchalance: you just let problems pass and move on w your life
  • Counternarrative
    • Chalance; caring about your problems and the problems of other people

Argument 1: Individual fulfillment

  • Pre-framing
    • This narrative affects the way you solve the most important problems you have, and the way it will affect you long-term
  • Analysis
    • More awareness of your problems
      • You feel the gravity of the problem -> You let yourself feel the full emotion of it, which is how you become aware of your own problems
      • On Side Opposition, you are more ignorant of your patterns -> Because you don’t think they are problems; you feel like they are “natural” and not negative
    • More incentive to fix your problems
      • You feel the stakes of your problems -> You obviously don’t wanna feel sad, and the more sad you feel the more you want to get out of it
      • On Side Opposition, you don’t want to fix your problems
      • > You feel more focused on moving on, even when your problems haven’t been fixed yet
      • > On Side Opposition, it looks like Nonchalance; you feel OA for going through improvement eras
    • More support structures to help you through your problems
      • People feel more empathetic to your problems when you tell them
      • They’re at least aware of whats going on in your life, they’re more likely to check on u and hang out with u
      • If they’re gen good friends, they’ll actually js feel bad and be better friends
      • Opp cf is that you have to deal with problems alone
      • You don’t wanna look insecure so you show that the problems aren’t affecting you
      • You gain distance from your friends because you’re sad and they don’t know why
    • More catharsis ⛓️☀️
      • The fact that you’re even processing your emotions is cathartic
      • Because you feel like you’re being honest with yourself
      • Because you find solutions to your problems when you say it out loud
      • You rant long enough to find what is actually bothering you, and you find the eureka moments that will help you
      • Opp cf is that you bottle in your emotions and relapse every now and then
      • Ranting is a sign of insecurity, better to just bottle it in now
  • Impacts
    • You move on from the problems you have easier
    • Makes you happier, you feel less bogged down by the probs you have, less traumatized
    • Makes you more productive, you just do the things you wanna do better
    • You have people to help you figure out your problems
    • You don’t know everything; you need people who can look at your problems objectively to help you out, or at least give you comfort when you can’t give comfort to yourself
  • Weighing
    • Biologically problems will still affect you
    • What’s important is that you can deal w them

Argument 2: Better collective action

  • Pre-framing
    • The way people care about bigger societal problems is if they can associate it with smaller, more personal problems
    • The people who care the most about BLM are those who see those problems in their neighbors, their friends, etc.
  • Analysis
    • People are more dismissive of other people’s problems
    • The norm promotes the idea that being vulnerable is kinda bad
    • You look at people voicing out their problems as a little random, or at worst, a little OA about their problems
    • You feel like the solutions to their problems are quite easy to find because you feel like you yourself aren’t going through anything, so why the hell is it so hard for other people to not go through anything
    • People are forced to care about your problems
    • Even if people give a shit, its because they have to / they look like a bad person for not giving a shit
    • Gov cf is where
      • People hear more stories about societal problems affecting indivs because people are more willing to tell the story
      • People are more empathetic to societal problems because they do not downplay it
  • Impact
    • Collective action efforts become weaker
    • People will not go out of their way to help if it isn’t their own problem
    • Even if they do, its unsustainable because people won’t stick around; as long as they’ve done the bare minimum, that’s fine
    • The healing of marginalized communities gets worse
    • Other people just don’t listen to you, invalidate your problems
    • At best you get the same blanket statements that tried to comfort you
  • Weighing
    • Opp is gonna try and say the narrative gives people the strength to move on and push forward
    • Things like racism will not change overnight — you can’t just sleep through racism
    • Things like racism affect you very deeply — you will still feel that problem even if you try to push it down
    • Our argument then matters because
      • It actually provides the direct way to solve these problems
      • If you cannot solve these problems, at least you fully realize them and get people to empathize enough to feel like those problems are valid
