Motion Analysis

This House Would...

This is a POLICY MOTION, based on whether or not we should implement/enact a certain policy.

Usually, the one implementing the policy is the government unless stated otherwise.

This House Believes that X should...

This is a POLICY MOTION, whether or not X should do Y or a certain action.

Government/Proposition teams can propose how Y would be done.

This House Believes That...

This is a VALUE JUDGEMENT MOTION, whether or not a statement is true.

It is not whether or not the government should do anything about the statement; it is about whether something is TRUE/FALSE.

This House Supports/Opposes...

In this motion type, teams will have to SUPPORT or OPPOSE X in the way it likely manifests in the world. Usually, these motions relate to one another. It is about supporting or opposing a certain idea in totality.

EXAMPLE: If a GOVERNMENT team supports the idea that X will do more harm than good. The OPPOSITION has to oppose the idea, where they believe X has done more good than harm.

This House Prefers...

Teams must defend a specific comparison provided. This may be done in two ways: “This House Prefers X over Y,” with the opposition defending Y over X, or “This House Prefers X,” with the opposition defending the status quo.

Opposition teams are burdened with defending their specific comparison and cannot defend something else or both (X & Y).

This House Would...

This is a POLICY MOTION on whether we should enact a certain policy, X. The one implementing/enacting said policy is the government/state, unless specified in the motion.

This House Prefers a world in which...

This is a UNIQUE type of THP motion. Government teams must defend the new world (world with X), while Opposition teams defend the status quo, or the world without X.

In this motion, teams must still analyze the timeline as to how the world developed with X.

This House Regrets...

In this motion type, teams must argue whether a world absent X would be better than the status quo. Government teams must argue against the status quo (REGRETTING X).

Opposition teams can set up what they defend, as long as it follows the lines of “This house does not regret X,” for the following reasons they ought to provide.

This House Predicts...

Teams must prove whether X will happen. It is not whether the object of prediction is good/preferable, but only whether it WILL happen.

Another way this motion type can be worded is “This House Believes That X will happen/occur”.
