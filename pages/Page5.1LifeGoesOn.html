Framing:

  • What is the narrative?
    • Justify problems you have as “natural” and part of life
    • Generally nonchalance: you just let problems pass and move on with your life
  • Counternarrative
    • Chalance; caring about your problems and the problems of others

Argument 1: Individual fulfillment

  • Pre-framing
    • This narrative affects the way you solve important problems and its long-term effect.
  • Analysis
    • Awareness of your problems and emotions
    • Incentives to fix your problems
    • Support structures from people around you
    • Catharsis from processing emotions
  • Impacts
    • Improved happiness and productivity
    • Better support from others

Argument 2: Better collective action

  • Pre-framing
    • People care about societal problems when connected to personal problems.
    • Some people are forced to care.
  • Analysis
    • People may be dismissive of other people's problems
    • Norms about vulnerability
    • Societal effects and empathy considerations
  • Impact
    • Collective action may weaken
    • People may not sustainably help
    • Marginalized communities affected
