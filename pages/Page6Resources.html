Additional Debate Resources

Here, we hope to add some additional resources, files, links, and drills/activities that may be helpful to beginner debaters!

Often, we've had trouble finding examples or showing people how to debate-- because everyone has their own distinct style and structure. Through this additional page, we hope to give people more inspiration or at the very least, a better understanding of how different people debate.

A. YouTube Channels & Debate Rounds

There are many YouTube channels that upload full debate rounds. These recordings help beginner debaters experience the flow, structure, and pacing of real debates.

B. Links for Different Motions

Websites such as debatedata.io and Calico Tabs provide large databases of past debate motions and competition statistics. These archives can be used for extra practice or brainstorming cases.

C. Additional YouTube Lectures

Video lectures can help explain speaker roles, case building, and motion analysis. These are useful for if you prefer watching and listening instead of reading text.

D. Drill Ideas

This section will include practice drills and activities that debaters can do in their free time to improve their skills.
