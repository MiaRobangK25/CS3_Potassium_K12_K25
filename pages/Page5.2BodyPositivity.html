Framing

  1. What does the shift look like?
    1. Internally: Public figures rapidly losing weight
    2. Externally: Promoting weight-loss products and encouraging people to lose weight
      1. The way they advocate for this is probably:
        1. It’s healthy
        2. It makes you pretty (quite on brand)
  2. What is the counternarrative?
    1. Body positivity
      1. Loving people for who they are and how their body looks

Arguments

  1. Why people who don’t fit weight beauty standards deserve support on Gov
    1. Why do people have a hard time losing weight?
      1. Medical conditions / disabilities
      2. Work or lack of time to commit to a diet
      3. Lack of money to pursue healthy options
      4. Social situations (e.g., culture, family eating habits)
        1. Implication: People often have no control over whether they fit weight norms
    2. How do we support them on Gov? (Manifestation)
      1. Allow them to love themselves through positive messaging
      2. Show media that glorifies and represents their traits
      3. Promote products that fit their traits (e.g., plus-sized clothes)
        1. These products don’t widely exist; visibility incentivizes creation
      4. Snowball effect: Other public figures also show support
    3. Counterfactual on Opp
      1. People who don’t fit these norms are guilted because they can’t adjust
        1. They lose representation
        2. They feel guilty for not being able to lose weight and feel it is their fault
  2. Why the shift incentivizes toxic weight loss
    1. Frame: There are already forces pushing people to consider losing weight
      1. Biological manifestations (e.g., physical limitations)
      2. Pressure from family and friends
    2. Why is the process worse on Opp?
      1. Idealistic portrayal — media presents weight loss as easy
        1. People feel bad for not being able to keep up
      2. Marketing based on beauty standards
        1. People compare themselves to thinner or fitter individuals
    3. Why is the process better on Gov?
      1. Weight loss becomes a personal choice
        1. No media guilt pushing bad decisions
        2. Change is based on personal fulfillment
      2. Healthier emotional process
        1. Less comparison with others
        2. Progress feels realistic; small steps feel fulfilling
  3. Public figures
    1. Reason
      1. Pressure to rebuild or maintain their brand
      2. Risk losing support from body-positive audiences
        1. They may appear inconsistent
      3. Pressure to be a role model and match weight-loss expectations
    2. Impact
      1. Public figures are still people and remain vulnerable
      2. If their health declines, their image declines and supporters are affected
