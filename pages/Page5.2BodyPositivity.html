Framing:

  • What does the shift look like?
    • Internally: Public figures rapidly losing weight
    • Externally: Promoting weight-loss products and encouraging people to lose weight
    • How they justify it:
      • It’s healthy
      • It makes you pretty (quite on brand)
  • Counternarrative
    • Body positivity
    • Loving people for who they are and how their body looks

Argument 1: Support for those who don’t fit weight beauty standards

  • Why people struggle to lose weight
    • Medical conditions / disabilities
    • Work or lack of time to commit to a diet
    • Lack of money to pursue healthy options
    • Social and cultural eating environments
    • Implication: Many people lack full control over whether they fit weight norms
  • Manifestation (Gov world)
    • Positive messaging that allows self-love
    • Media that glorifies and represents diverse traits
    • Promotion of inclusive products (e.g., plus-sized clothes)
    • Incentivizes companies to create more inclusive products
    • Snowball effect: Other public figures also show support
  • Counterfactual on Opp
    • People are guilted for not fitting norms
    • They lose representation
    • They internalize blame for not losing weight

Argument 2: Why the shift incentivizes toxic weight loss

  • Pre-framing
    • There are already forces pushing people to consider losing weight
    • Biological manifestations (e.g., physical limitations)
    • Pressure from family and friends
  • Why the process is worse on Opp
    • Media portrays weight loss as easy and idealistic
    • People feel bad for not keeping up
    • Marketing is based on beauty standards
    • Encourages harmful comparison to thinner or fitter individuals
  • Why the process is better on Gov
    • Weight loss becomes a personal choice
    • Less guilt-driven decision making
    • Change is based on personal fulfillment
    • Less comparison with others
    • Progress feels realistic and fulfilling

Argument 3: Public figures

  • Reasons for the shift
    • Pressure to rebuild or maintain their brand
    • Risk losing support from body-positive audiences
    • May appear inconsistent
    • Pressure to be a role model and match weight-loss expectations
  • Impact
    • Public figures are still vulnerable individuals
    • If their health declines, their image declines
    • Supporters are negatively affected
