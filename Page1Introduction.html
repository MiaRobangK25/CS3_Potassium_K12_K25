Debate

The world of debate, brought closer to you ;>

1. The Most Popular Debate Formats

The Philippine Debate Circuit is very competitive. Debate is a sport, a sport a lot of people take seriously-- or find solace in. Yet, at the same time, debate is also very flexible! In the Philippine world of debate-- there are two popular formats. The first is A] ASIAN PARLIAMENTARY (3v3), and B] BRITISH PARLIAMENTARY (2v2v2v2). In a debate round (or room), it is made up of a team of 3 versus another team of 3 in Asian Parliamentary-- and for British Parliamentary, it is 4 teams made up of pairs all competing for being 1st.

2. Sides of a Debate, and Preparation Time

Aside from talking about the two formats in the Philippine Debate World, we want to also explain the technicalities of debate. There is a side FOR the motion, and a side AGAINST the motion. These sides are referred to as PROPOSITION/GOVERNMENT (FOR) and OPPOSITION (AGAINST) respectively.

Both sides will also have a set amount of PREPARATION TIME (Prep Time) to create their case as to why they are FOR or AGAINST the motion. These cases will be the determining factor as who wins the debate, which you can check out in one of the other pages in this website! Before the debate round itself, the motion will be released-- and all teams will have a certain amount of time to prepare, then go straight into all of the speeches afterwards. In ASIAN PARLIAMENTARY, both teams have 30 minutes to prepare for their cases and speeches! As for BRITISH PARLIAMENTARY, the 4 teams only have 15 minutes to prepare for their speeches and the round.

3. Speeches and Speaker Positions

Each person/debater in the debate has 7 minutes and 15 minutes to speak! You're not required to fill up the entire 7 minutes and 15 seconds, but if you have a lot to say-- you can. For Asian Parliamentary as well, there is a REPLY speaker in the FLOW of the debate/round (which will be discussed in the 4th Web Page), which will only speak for a maximum of 4 minutes.

There are also different speaker positions for each debate format>, and the speaker role/position still depends on what position you and your team choose before the round. Each of these speaker positions have different goals/purposes, and it really depends on what ROLE best suits you!

4. Quick Overview of Motions

We've briefly explained this in 1., when we explained how some teams are FOR/AGAINST the motion! Although, there still so much left to talk about for motions.

For now, since this is supposed to be a quick introduction/overview, what you need to know is that debate motions can vary! All motions are worded in the format of "TH", which stands for "THIS HOUSE". Some motions can be written in the form of "THIS HOUSE REGRETS X" for example, or "THIS HOUSE, AS [PERSON]", with many more variants. For now, just remember-- this house doesn't mean you're debating as a house... most motions are debating FOR/AGAINST the motion in terms of an observers' perspective.
